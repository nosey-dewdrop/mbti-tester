so you met someone. you like them. or you're stuck in a group project with them. either way — what's their deal?

you can't hand them a 16personalities link. that's weird. so instead, you observe.

imagine you're at a party. some people are in the center, telling stories, laughing loud. others are in the corner, having one deep conversation.

that's the first clue.

🌎

some people talk about what happened — facts, details, the real world. others talk about what could happen — ideas, patterns, possibilities.

that's the second clue.

⚖️

when a friend makes a terrible decision — do you say "here's why that's dumb" or "i understand how you feel"?

head vs heart. third clue.

📅

plans change last minute. some people lose it. others say "cool, what's the new plan?"

structure vs flow. fourth and final clue.

🧹

4 axes. 2 options each. 16 combinations.

that's mbti. it's not a horoscope. it's not a diagnosis. it's a lens — and a surprisingly useful one when you're trying to figure out why someone acts the way they do.

the four axes

how people take in the world and make decisions

E energy I

extraversion

  • talks to think
  • energized by people
  • wide circle, many friends
  • acts first, reflects later

introversion

  • thinks to talk
  • drained by crowds
  • small circle, deep bonds
  • reflects first, acts later
spot it: after a long group hangout, do they want more or need to recharge alone?
S information N

sensing

  • focuses on what IS
  • practical, concrete
  • remembers details
  • trusts experience

intuition

  • focuses on what COULD BE
  • abstract, theoretical
  • sees patterns
  • trusts gut feelings
spot it: when they tell a story, do they describe exactly what happened or what it meant?
T decisions F

thinking

  • logic first, always
  • values fairness
  • blunt honesty
  • debates for fun

feeling

  • values first, always
  • values harmony
  • tactful honesty
  • avoids conflict
spot it: when you vent about a problem, do they give you a solution or a hug?
J lifestyle P

judging

  • plans everything
  • loves closure
  • early is on time
  • decides fast

perceiving

  • improvises everything
  • keeps options open
  • on time is early
  • decides... eventually
spot it: you change plans last minute. are they stressed or excited?

combine all four and you get one of 16 types. an INTJ is introverted, intuitive, thinking, judging — the strategic mastermind who will sacrifice you for the greater good. an ESFP is extraverted, sensing, feeling, perceiving — the life of the party who lives entirely in the moment.
